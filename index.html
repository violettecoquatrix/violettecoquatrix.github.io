The Pattern

Everyone celebrates growth.
Few prepare for what it breaks.

I've seen the same pattern across industries and continents. Demand accelerates. Operations lag. Inventory grows faster than clarity. Decisions slow down. Teams compensate with heroics. On the surface, things look fine. Underneath, execution quietly becomes the risk.

↓

Margins eroding

Revenue grows, but costs grow faster. Nobody can explain where the money goes.

↻

Decisions stalling

Everything escalates to the founder. The org can't move without one person in the room.

↖

Systems lagging

Data doesn't match reality. Finance and Ops tell different stories. Nobody trusts the numbers.

⊕

Teams stretched thin

Good people burning out. Heroics instead of systems. Execution depends on individuals, not process.

Approach

I don't babysit operations.
I build the system that runs them.

Most companies don't need another manager. They need someone who can see the full picture, design the right operating structure, install it, and leave. I work in close partnership with Finance to make sure Ops executes and the numbers are real. I don't replace your CFO. I make operations behave so Finance can trust them.

Fit

This works when the match is right.

● I work well with

  • Scale-ups where growth has outpaced operational foundations
  • Founders who want real answers, not comfort
  • Companies between $5M and $150M in revenue, 15 to 500 people
  • PE-backed or post-Series B environments with board pressure
  • Teams with high standards, directness, and a preference for context over control
  • SaaS, e-commerce, CPG, and industrial businesses with operational depth

○ I don't work well with

  • Highly political organizations where truth is optional
  • Cultures that reward mediocrity or docility
  • Companies that want slides instead of decisions
  • Environments where presence matters more than impact
  • Teams looking for a babysitter, not a builder
