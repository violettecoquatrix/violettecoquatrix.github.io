The Pattern

Everyone celebrates growth.
Few prepare for what it breaks.

I've seen the same pattern across industries and continents. Demand accelerates. Operations lag. Inventory grows faster than clarity. Decisions slow down. Teams compensate with heroics. On the surface, things look fine. Underneath, execution quietly becomes the risk.

When founders feel this, they default to one of four moves: hire a full-time COO they can't yet afford, call a consulting firm that delivers slides, ask their investor's operating partner for a favor, or do nothing and hope it sorts itself out. None of these solve the actual problem, because the problem is rarely what it looks like. Four symptoms screaming at once almost always trace back to one constraint. Find that, fix that, and the rest starts to resolve.

↓

Where AI actually works in ops

Demand forecasting. Automated reporting. Scenario modeling. Workflow automation for tasks that eat your team's time but don't need human judgment. AI is great at these. It's terrible at leadership, culture, and deciding what matters. I know the difference, and I won't sell you the parts that don't work.
